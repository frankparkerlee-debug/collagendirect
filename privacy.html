Privacy Policy

Last Updated: October 29, 2025

CollagenDirect ("we," "us," "our," or "Company") is committed to protecting the privacy of our users. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard information when you use our website (collagendirect.health) and our Provider Portal (collectively, the "Platform").

1. Scope and Acceptance

This Privacy Policy applies to:

  • Healthcare providers, physicians, practice managers, and DME suppliers ("Providers")
  • Patients whose information is entered into our Platform by Providers
  • Visitors to our public website

By registering for, accessing, or using the Platform, you acknowledge and agree to this Privacy Policy. If you are a Provider, you represent and warrant that you have obtained all necessary authorizations and consents from your patients to share their information through the Platform and for the purposes described herein.

2. Marketing Communications Consent

IMPORTANT: SMS and Email Marketing Consent

By using the Platform, Providers expressly consent and agree that:

  • Provider Consent: You (the Provider, physician, practice manager, or authorized representative) consent to receive marketing communications from CollagenDirect, MD DME LLC, and our affiliated partners via email, SMS text messages, and phone calls. This includes promotional materials, product updates, educational content, special offers, and other marketing communications.
  • Patient Consent on Behalf of Patients: By entering patient information into the Platform and ordering products for patients, you represent that you have obtained appropriate authorization from your patients, and you consent on behalf of your patients to allow CollagenDirect and MD DME LLC to send marketing communications to those patients via email and SMS text messages regarding wound care products, treatment options, reorder reminders, and related healthcare information.
  • Scope of Communications: Marketing communications may include:
    • Product information and educational materials about wound care treatments
    • Reorder reminders and supply notifications
    • Special offers, promotions, and discounts
    • New product announcements
    • Healthcare tips and best practices
    • Surveys and feedback requests
    • Account updates and platform enhancements
  • SMS Text Messaging: You understand that standard message and data rates may apply to SMS messages. Message frequency may vary. You can opt out of SMS messages at any time by replying STOP to any text message. Reply HELP for assistance.
  • Autodialed and Prerecorded Messages: You consent to receive autodialed, prerecorded, and/or artificial voice messages at the phone numbers you provide, even if the number is on a Do Not Call registry.
  • Not a Condition of Service: Consent to receive marketing communications is not a condition of purchasing products or using the Platform for order fulfillment purposes. However, opting out of communications may limit your ability to receive important product updates, reorder reminders, and promotional offers.

Opt-Out Rights

You have the right to opt out of marketing communications at any time:

  • Email: Click the "unsubscribe" link at the bottom of any marketing email
  • SMS: Reply STOP to any text message
  • Phone: Request removal from call lists during any call or contact us at privacy@collagendirect.health
  • Account Settings: Update your communication preferences in the Provider Portal

Please note that even if you opt out of marketing communications, we may still send you transactional and operational messages related to your orders, account, and service (such as order confirmations, shipping updates, and security alerts).

3. Information We Collect

a. Provider Information

When you register or use the Platform, we collect:

  • Personal identifiers: Name, email address, phone number, business address
  • Professional credentials: NPI number, medical license number and state, DEA number (if applicable), DME license information
  • Practice information: Practice name, tax ID, specialty, affiliated physicians
  • Account credentials: Username, password (encrypted), security questions
  • Payment and billing information: Bank details, credit card information (processed by third-party payment processors)
  • Usage data: Login history, IP addresses, device information, browser type, pages visited, features used

b. Patient Information (PHI)

Providers may enter Protected Health Information (PHI) about their patients, including:

  • Personal identifiers: Name, date of birth, address, phone number, email address
  • Health information: Diagnosis codes (ICD-10), wound assessments, treatment plans, medical history
  • Insurance information: Payer details, policy numbers, Medicare/Medicaid numbers
  • Treatment records: Product orders, quantities, delivery addresses, prescription information
  • Clinical documentation: Wound photographs, progress notes, Standard Written Orders

HIPAA Compliance: Patient PHI is protected under HIPAA and handled according to our Business Associate Agreement (BAA). We use administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect PHI.

c. Automatically Collected Information

  • Cookies and tracking technologies: Session cookies, preference cookies, analytics cookies
  • Log data: Access times, page views, referring URLs, error logs
  • Device information: Operating system, screen resolution, unique device identifiers

d. Third-Party Information

  • NPPES database verification (NPI validation)
  • Medicare/Medicaid provider enrollment verification
  • License verification through state medical boards
  • Credit verification services (for payment terms approval)

We share information with third parties who perform services on our behalf:

  • Customer Support Tools: Zendesk, Intercom, or similar platforms manage support tickets and live chat.

All third-party service providers are contractually obligated to protect your information and use it only for the purposes we specify. Providers handling PHI execute Business Associate Agreements.

b. Legal Requirements and Safety

We may disclose information when required by law or to protect rights and safety:

  • In response to subpoenas, court orders, or legal process

  • To report suspected fraud, abuse, or illegal activity to authorities
  • To protect against harm to the rights, property, or safety of CollagenDirect, our users, or the public
  • In connection with government investigations or regulatory inquiries

If CollagenDirect is involved in a merger, acquisition, asset sale, or bankruptcy, your information may be transferred to the successor entity. We will provide notice and obtain consent where required by law.

d. With Your Consent

We may share information with third parties when you explicitly consent or direct us to do so.

e. De-Identified and Aggregated Data

We may share de-identified, aggregated data that cannot reasonably identify individuals for research, analytics, marketing, or business purposes. For example, "85% of practices order collagen sheets monthly" or "Average wound closure time is 21 days."

6. Data Security and Retention

a. Security Measures

We implement industry-standard security practices to protect your information:

  • Encryption: All data transmitted between your browser and our servers uses TLS 1.2+ encryption. PHI stored in our database is encrypted at rest using AES-256.
  • Access Controls: Role-based access controls (RBAC) ensure employees and service providers access only the data necessary for their functions.
  • Authentication: Secure password requirements, multi-factor authentication (MFA) for administrative accounts, and session timeout policies.
  • Audit Logs: We maintain comprehensive audit trails of all PHI access, modifications, and disclosures as required by HIPAA.
  • Penetration Testing: Regular security assessments and vulnerability scanning.
  • Employee Training: All employees complete HIPAA and data security training.

No system is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security. You are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of your account credentials.

b. Data Retention

We retain information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required by law:

  • Provider Account Data: Retained for the duration of your account plus 7 years after termination (to comply with healthcare record retention requirements).
  • Patient PHI: Retained for 7 years from the date of last service (or longer if required by state law).
  • Financial Records: Retained for 7 years per IRS and Medicare requirements.
  • Audit Logs: Retained for 7 years per HIPAA requirements.
  • Marketing Data: Retained until you opt out or request deletion, then deleted within 30 days.

After retention periods expire, we securely delete or de-identify information.
